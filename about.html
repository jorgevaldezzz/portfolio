About

I'm drawn to work that sits close to how systems actually behave, rather than stopping at the surface of what they're supposed to do. That shows up in how I approach software: I want to understand how it's structured, how it holds up under real or adversarial conditions, and where reliability and security stop being assumptions and start being decisions someone has to make.

Most of my project work traces back to that interest. I care about clear implementation over clever implementation, documentation that actually explains a decision instead of just describing it, and treating a project as something to iterate on rather than finish once and walk away from. That last part matters more than it sounds — a lot of what I've learned came from going back into a working project and asking why a specific choice held up or didn't.
